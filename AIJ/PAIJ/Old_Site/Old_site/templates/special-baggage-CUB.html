Special baggage

Musical Instruments

If you’re taking musical instruments with you, they can fly as carry-on baggage if they are packed in a hard case that doesn’t exceed the following dimensions: 40 x 30 x 22 cm (15 x 12 x 9 in) and their maximum weight is 10 kg (22 lb).

If your musical instruments exceed the limits of the carry-on baggage, they will have to fly as checked baggage. The suitcase should not exceed 2 m (78 in) in length and weight no more than 30 kg (66 lb).

In case of delicate or voluminous musical instruments, they may occupy an additional seat, involving a full fare seat payment. Musical instruments should not exceed 2 m (78 in) in length or weight more than 46 kg (101 lb).

Sports equipment

Depending on the type of sport equipment you carry, it is important to consider the restrictions of weight and length in order to be transported by Interjet. Also, please take into account the following specifications:

  • Golf equipment It consist in a golf bag containing clubs, golf balls and one pair of golf shoes. These items count as one piece of baggage and must be suitably packed in rigid and/or hard shell container specifically designed for shipping.
  • Fishing equipment You can travel with a fishing rod with a maximum length of 3.5 m (138 in), reel, fishing lines (nylon, dyneema or spectra), hooks, lures, baits, weights, corks and a fishing net. All equipment must be transported in a rigid case or container bag.
  • Scuba gear You can bring an empty tank of less than 40 PSI, manometer, a snorkel, a harpoon, a pair of fins, safety vest, a weight belt, a regulator and a diving mask.
  • Surfboard You may choose to travel with a surfboard, kitesurfing equipment or a boogie board. In either case the maximum length shall be
    3.5 m (138 in).
  • Hunting gear Only may be transported those that are not considered as firearms (ARPEL).

Mobility aids

In addition to the regular baggage allowance, Interjet will accept, without charge, as priority checked baggage, mobility aids, including:

  • An electric wheelchair (with non-spillable batteries only)
  • A scooter or a manually operated rigid-frame wheelchair
  • A manually operated folding wheelchair
  • A walker, a cane, crutches or braces
  • Any device that assists the person to communicate, and
  • Any prosthesis or medical device.

None of the previously mentioned items are subject to our bagagge policy, which means that can be transported at no cost. However, due to space limitations, these objects can only be transported as checked baggage.

Pasajeros que viajan a Canadá deberán tramitar su eTA

Por disposición de las autoridades canadienses, aquellos pasajeros extranjeros exentos del visado deberán tramitar su eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) para facilitar su entrada al país. Para mayor información, el gobierno canadiense pone a disposición la siguiente página para la realización del trámite: http://www.cic.gc.ca/english/visit/eta-start-es.asp
