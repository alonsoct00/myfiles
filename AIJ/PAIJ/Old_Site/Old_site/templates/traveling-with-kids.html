Traveling with kids

Children under two years

This protection includes:

  • Infants (children under two years of age on the date of travel) do not require a seat and may travel in the lap of an accompanying adult (18 years of age or older). In such cases, passengers need to inform Interjet that they are traveling with an infant at the moment of the purchase. The airline will issue, free of charge, a ticket and boarding pass for the infant but without a baggage allowance entitlement.
  • Some countries charge a departure tax. In those cases, infants don’t pay a fare but have to pay the departure tax of the country of origin.
  • Only one infant under the age of two years old may be held in the lap of an accompanying adult passenger.
  • An adult may only travel with two infants. The second one will have to pay a fare and has the right to baggage allowance and his/her own seat.
  • Infants under two years of age at the time of departure but reaching his/her second birthday during the continuing/return flight(s) will require a seat and must pay the applicable fare for the continuing/return flight(s).
  • An infant under two years of age occupying a seat must be properly secured in an approved child restraint device.
  • All persons of two years of age or older must be ticketed, assigned a seat, and pay the applicable fare.

Documents

The accompanying passenger, when checking in for their flight, must provide official government documentation (birth certificate) verifying the age of the infant and/or child.
For safety reasons, Interjet reserves the right to not transport infants less than seven (7) days old.

In addition to the above, Interjet may require presentation of the following documents when children are accompanied by an adult:

  • Documentation establishing legal custody.
  • Parental consent letter authorizing travel.
  • Any other documentation required by the country of destination.

Nursing mothers

Nursing mothers are invited to breastfeed or pump in our aircraft. If you need to use a breast pump, we recommend using a battery-operated or a manual one, because the availability of inflight seat power varies by aircraft fleet.

Breast pumps can be carried on board in addition to the one bag and one personal item limit. Due to the unpredictability of turbulence, we are not able to allow nursing mothers to pump in the aircraft galley areas or to use jump seats.

Our flight attendants may be able to provide hot water or ice to keep items hot or cold, but they are not permitted to store items such as milk or formula.

Inflight amenities

Food

If you are traveling with an infant, please bring enough food and supplies with you. In case you give formula to your baby, remember to take only the necessary quantity for the flight, making sure you don’t exceed the 100 ml accepted on carry-on baggage.

Interjet on-board service provides your children with juices, soft drinks or water throughout the flight, as well as sweet or salty snacks, depending on the flight schedule. Get more details here.

Changing tables

Changing tables are available in all the lavatories of our fleet.

Strollers and car seats

Strollers and car seats are not subject to our baggage policy, which means that can be transported at no cost. However, due to space limitations, these items can only be transported as checked baggage.

Para su comodidad, las carriolas y las sillas de bebés pueden llevarse hasta la puerta del avión, en donde deben ser entregadas al personal de la aerolínea para que se transporten en el compartimiento de equipaje.

General Tips

  • Arrive at the airport on time.
  • Travel in comfortable clothing.
  • Walk with them before boarding.
  • Give them something to eat or drink when the plane is taking off and landing, this can relieve ear pain caused by cabin pressure adjusting to the altitude.
  • Remember to keep their toys, books or a tablet with appropriated contents nearby, for quick access to them.
  • Try to schedule nap times to coincide with the flight.
